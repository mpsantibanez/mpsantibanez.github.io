I am an applied microeconomist interested in child well-being, family background, labor economics, and economic history. My current research examines the relationship between parental contact with the justice system and adolescent offending in Chile, as part of a broader agenda on children's life trajectories and intergenerational disadvantage.

I am a PhD Candidate in Economics at the [University of Chile](https://www.fen.uchile.cl/), supported by the ANID National Doctoral Fellowship. Before my PhD, I received a Master of Arts in Economic Policy from Boston University and a B.Sc. in Commercial Engineering from Universidad Técnica Federico Santa María, where I continue to teach courses in economics and was recognized as Best Professor 2022 by my students.

I am based in Santiago, Chile. Feel free to reach out at [marsantiba@fen.uchile.cl](mailto:marsantiba@fen.uchile.cl).
