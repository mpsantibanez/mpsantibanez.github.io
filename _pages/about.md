I am an applied microeconomist interested in child well-being, family background, labor economics, and economic history. My current research examines the relationship between parental contact with the justice system and adolescent offending in Chile, as part of a broader agenda on children's life trajectories and intergenerational disadvantage.

I am a 4th year PhD student in Economics at the [University of Chile](https://www.fen.uchile.cl/). Before my PhD, I received a M.A. in Economic Policy from Boston University and a B.A. in Business and Economics from Universidad Técnica Federico Santa María.
