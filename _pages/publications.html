The Intergenerational Transmission of Crime: The Role of Parental Criminal Behavior
Working Paper, 2026

This paper examines the intergenerational transmission of criminal behavior in a developing economy. Using a novel administrative dataset covering the full birth cohort of children born in Chile in 2010, I estimate the association between parental contact with the justice system and adolescent offending through a Linear Probability Model with school and commune fixed effects. I document four dimensions of heterogeneity: offense severity, timing of exposure (strongest at ages 11–14), gender and parental structure, and offense type. Parental incarceration is associated with offending rates approximately 500% above the control mean. The results suggest that intergenerational crime transmission is concentrated among specific high-risk family profiles.
