**Abstract**

This paper examines the intergenerational transmission of criminal behavior in the context of a developing economy. Using a novel administrative dataset covering the full cohort of children born in Chile in 2010, I estimate the conditional association between parental contact with the justice system and adolescent offending through a Linear Probability Model with school and commune fixed effects.
